This month: Liege, Belgium

Situated in the valley of the Meuse River, not far from Belgium's borders with the Netherlands and Germany, Liege is the economic and cultural center of the country's French-speaking Walloon region. This is the chief arrival point for drivers and aficionados flying in for championship auto races at the Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps.

5 things you should know about Liege

1 Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps: This nearby race track is the celebrated venue
  of the F1 Belgian Grand Prix and the Spa 24 Hours endurance race.
2 Musee de la Vie Wallonne: 350,000 exhibits and documents illustrate life
  and culture in the Walloon part (southern region) of Belgium.
3 Mediacite: Designed by renowned architect-designer Ron Arad,
  Mediacite is a must-visit, multi-functional complex combining commerce,
  leisure, culture, media and economic activity.
4 Blegny-Mine: Discover Wallonia's rich industrial heritage with a short
  trip to this nearby coal mine museum.
5 Prince-Bishops' Palace: Victor Hugo once said of it: "Nowhere have I
  seen a construction so remarkable, serious and grandiose at the same time."
