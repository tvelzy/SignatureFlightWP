This month: Munich

Munich is ranked as one of top ten best cities to live in the world. It has the sophistication of a big-city but on a smaller and much friendlier scale. Quiet and spacious with wide boulevards, large squares and well-kept parks, it is a delight to walk around. Munch is also an important cultural center of Europe: known for its lively theatre scene it has the largest number of small independent theatre houses in the country. With more than enough attractions to satisfy the most demanding visitor's appetite, Munich serves as the perfect base from which to explore Bavaria and beyond.

Things you can do

1 Deutsches Museum: The largest museum of technology and natural sciences in
  the world, with over 13 acres of exhibitions containing invaluable original machines
  and equipment, models and reconstructions, from classical mechanics to
  telecommunications. Open Monday to Saturday from 9:00 am to 6:00 pm.
2 The English Garden (Englischer Garten): Created by Prince Elector Karl Theodor
  in 1789, this is Europe's largest city park, featuring vast meadows, ancient forests,
  streams, ponds and a beautiful lake. Open all year round.
3 Marienplatz: The city's central square considered by Munichites, as the front room
  or Guten Stube of the city, features the spectacular Glockenspiel, an animated clock
  in the tower of the Neue Rathaus (New City Hall).
4 Neuschwanstein Castle: Situated just 2 hours drive from the city, is possibly the
  most famous castle in the world. Built between 1868 - 1886 by King Ludwig II of
  Bavaria (though he never lived to see its completion), the castle is decorated with
  lavish frescoes of scenes from Wagner's operas and the armory contains a fine
  collection of suits formerly belonging to Henry II of France. Open daily from 10:00 am
  to 4:00 pm.

MUC: Munich International Airport

Located just 20 minutes drive from the city center, Munich International is Germany's 2nd largest after Frankfurt and has won best international airport 4 times in a row. If you are an enthusiast, the airport has three historic aircraft on display: a Super Constellation, a Douglas DC-3 and a Junkers Ju 52/3m. From the airport, a 45 minute train ride, using suburban railway lines S1 and S8 will take you into the city center. If you are driving, the A 92 motorway gives easy access to the A 9 and Munich's A 99 ring road.
