This month: Anchorage

Anchorage is the largest city in Alaska with a population of 300,000. The breathtaking scenery, diverse wildlife (including Grizzly bears) and limitless outdoor recreational opportunities attract thousands of visitors each year adding to the city’s cosmopolitan atmosphere. Summer is long and dry with temperatures averaging 16 °C / 60 °F, allowing you ample time to enjoy the dazzling displays of flowers, live music and salmon fishing downtown in Ship Creek.

Things you can do in Anchorage

1 Anchorage Museum photo exhibition: ‘All That Glitters: Images from the Alaska
  Gold Rush’ presents stories of prospectors: their strategies and obstacles,
  disappointments and windfalls. Open daily from 9am - 6pm until Sep 25, 2011.
2 Music in the Park: Enjoy a free outdoor concert and mingle with the local population
  each day in Peratrovich Park from noon - 1pm.
3 Christmas in July: A special one-day event held at the Egan Center, mixing art and
  Christmas carol singing. Held on July 16 from 10am - 6pm.
4 Alaska Salsa Festival 2011: Join the hundreds of dance enthusiasts or see one of
  the many performances at this weekend long dance festival. Held at the Captain Cook
  Hotel from 21 - 24 July. Doors open at 10am.
5 8th Annual Cardboard Boat Regatta: Possibly one of the most bizarre regattas
  you’ll ever experience. If you wish to participate then note that duct tape and/or white
  glue must be used to keep you boat together. Races begin at 1pm on July 23 on
  Goose lake.
6 Anchorage Farmers Market: The only non-profit, farmer-directed market in
  Anchorage held daily between 9am - 2pm at 15th & Cordova until mid-October.

ANC: Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport

Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport (ANC) is Alaska’s major airport. A free airport shuttle bus takes passengers to the South (Domestic) Terminal, North (International) Terminal and Rental Car Facility daily, every 15 minutes. If you want a car then plan ahead as most rental companies are pretty much sold out from mid-June through the end of August. Otherwise there is a good bus service into the city and a taxi ride shouldn’t cost you more than $25.
