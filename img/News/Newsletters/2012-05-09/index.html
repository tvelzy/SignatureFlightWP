This month: Frankfurt am Main

Originally one of the most important cities in the Roman Empire, Frankfurt Am Main, commonly known as Frankfurt, has lost none of its grandeur. Sometimes humorously referred to as Mainhattan, the city features some of the highest, most avant-garde skyscrapers of Europe, that together form a unique and impressive skyline. In stark contrast, Frankfurt's historical center (the Altstadt) with its middle-aged style buildings is mostly reconstructed.

Things you can do

1 The Städel: One of Germany's most important and oldest art collections. Founded in 1815, the Städel invites visitors to undertake a journey through the cultural landscapes of Europe from the Middle Ages to the present.
2 Goethe House and Goethe Museum: Birthplace of Johann Wolfgang von Goethe (1710 – 1782), one of the greatest German authors of the 19th century, features an impressive collection of classical paintings. Open Monday to Saturday from 10 am to 6 pm and on Sunday from 10 am to 5:30 pm.
3 Frankfurt Opera: Premier showcase for world-class opera and ballet currently presents 'The Rake's Progress'. Sung in English, this is one of Igor Stravinsky's greatest works. Presented May 24, 26 and 28.
4 Botanical Garden (Palmengarten): A veritable journey through the plant kingdom, from the hardwood forests of North America to the jungle of South-East Asia. Open daily from 9 am to 6 pm.
5 Waldchestag (Forest Day): A unique Frankfurt custom and a public holiday, whereupon both young and old head out to the City Forest, the largest of its kind in Germany, for 3 days of frivolities. From May 26 through the 29.
 

FRA: Frankfurt Airport

Frankfurt Airport is the biggest German airport and among the busiest in Europe - third in passenger traffic after London Heathrow and Paris Charles de Gaulle. The airport is connected to downtown Frankfurt by taxi, bus (Line 61 to Frankfurt Südbahnhof) and most easily by S-Bahn (fast commuter trains). The train ride from the airport to the city center takes approximately 14 minutes. Alternatively if you choose to drive yourself, the A3, A67 and A5 motorways all lead directly into Frankfurt.
