Newark, New Jersey

Nicknamed the "Gateway City", Newark is one of the country's major transportation hubs with plenty of options in air, shipping and rail centers. People are always pleasantly surprised to learn that there is plenty to do in this animated city, from festivals and parades, to museums and concert venues. 9 miles (14 km) west of New York City, this thriving epicenter is a great entryway to the US Open and NYC.

 
 
 

Things you can do:

  1. US Open 2012 Tennis Tournament: The US Open Tennis Tournament is upon us. From August 27 to September 9, you can catch matches day or evening at Arthur Ash Stadium, in Flushing Meadows Queens, New York. Group sales, corporate hospitality packages and individual tickets can be purchased on the US Open official website. Transportation to and from the event is easily accessible from Newark Airport by rail and shuttle.

     

  2. Newark Museum: Once located in the Newark Public Library, this museum has since expanded and moved to house some of the most extensive collections of American art.  Additionally, the museum lends its focus to natural sciences, with facilities that include the Dreyfuss Planetarium and the Victoria Hall of Science.  Open Wednesday to Sunday 12 noon to 5 pm. Closed Mondays.
 
 

EWR Newark Liberty Intl Airport

Newark Liberty Intl Airport has become one of the world's busiest airports (handling 33.8 million passengers in 2011). In preparation for the 2014 Super Bowl at the Meadowlands, the airport has received $70 million in investments to support significant improvements over the next 2 years.  The new upgrades are set to include a new maintenance hangar provided by United Airlines, and Signature Flight Support Corp. will build a new passenger terminal and renovate ramps.  The airport offers easy access to the US Open and is just 9 miles (14 km) from New York City. Additional airport bases offering access to this event are MMU, TEB, and HPN.
