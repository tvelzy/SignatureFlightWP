This month: Cardiff, Wales

Cardiff is the capital, largest city and main commercial center of Wales. Caerdydd, as the Welsh call it, is undergoing a major redevelopment and was, in 2009, presented the European City Of Sport award thanks to its increasingly important role in hosting major international sporting events, like this year's Ryder Cup.

5 things you should know about Cardiff

1 Ryder Cup 2010: This golf competition between teams from Europe and the US
  takes place in Wales this year. If you're flying in to participate or attend,
  PPR is required. All requests can be registered through Signature Cardiff.
2 Cardiff Castle: 2000 years of history packed into an eight acre site:
  Roman, Norman, Plantaganet and Victorian.
3 St. Fagans, The National History Museum: A 100-acre, open-air museum featuring
  rescued, restored and authentically furnished buildings from all over Wales.
4 National Museum and Art Gallery: This art museum contains a wonderful collection
  of Impressionist and Post-Impressionist art; it also contains a gallery
  devoted to contemporary Welsh artists.
5 Lloyds TSB Cardiff Half Marathon: Considered one of the best half marathons
  in the UK, this race promises a stunning scenery and carnival atmosphere.

CWL: Cardiff International Airport

Cardiff International Airport is located just 35 minutes by road from the bustling city of Cardiff. With Cardiff Castle, the Millennium Stadium, and Cardiff International Arena in the city center, and the Wales Millennium Centre at Cardiff Bay, plus a wealth of bars, clubs, restaurants and shops, your visit can be as active or relaxing as you wish.
