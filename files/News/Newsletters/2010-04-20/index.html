This month: Cape Town, South Africa

As the second most-populous city of South Africa and home to the much anticipated 2010 World Cup, Cape Town is renowned for its Mediterranean climate, gleaming harbor, award-winning vineyards and a stunning skyline dominated by the iconic Table Mountain.

5 things you should know about Cape Town

1 World Cup Football: From 11 June to 11 July, South Africa hosts World Cup
  Football, marking the first time this championship has ever been held on
African soil.
2 Victoria & Albert Waterfront: Where else will you have a mountain behind
  you and an ocean before you? Shop, dine, sail, and dance to local rhythms.
3 Mount Nelson Hotel's Planet and Champagne Bar: For five-star glamour
  and to see and be seen, Capetonians head here.
4 Constantia Valley: A rural retreat and the departure point to explore South
  Africa's celebrated wine route.
5 African Aerospace and Defense: AAD, from 21-25 September, is a showcase
  for civil and military aviation, maritime and land-based defense technologies.

CPT - FACT: Cape Town International Airport

Cape Town International Airport is Africa's 3rd largest airport. Located approximately 20 kilometers (12 mi) from the city center, it was opened in 1954 and is currently undergoing major construction upgrades in anticipation of increased traffic volumes associated with the World Cup tournament in June.
