What's on in Dublin

 

Where better to be on St. Patrick's Day than Dublin, a city bursting with optimism, energy and rich history. One thing travelers will discover right away is Dublin's welcoming nature, where locals love to share stories of the city's past. Walk down fabled O'Connell Street and a friendly face will recall the history of the General Post Office, where the markings from battles past still remain on the facade. Hankering for live Irish music? Check out Temple Bar, where the pubs are always packed and the Celtic classics are sung until the wee hours.

 
 
 
 

Top Attractions in Dublin:

  1. The Old Library and Book of Kells Exhibit: Step back in time on the campus of Trinity College and visit the Book of Kells exhibit, an ancient manuscript containing the four Gospels in Latin. While there, stroll along the walkways of The Old Library, where marble busts line the stacks of bookshelves containing classic Irish literary works.

  2. Temple Bar: Along the south bank of the River Liffey lies Dublin's renowned Temple Bar neighborhood. The cobblestone streets of Temple Bar come alive at night, as visitors and locals flock to the many Irish pubs that dot the medieval streetscape here.

  3. The Guinness Storehouse: A visit to Dublin isn't complete until you savor a sip of Ireland's national drink, Guinness Beer. Stop by St. James Gate and take the Guinness Storehouse tour, which wraps up with a free taste at the glass-lined top floor bar. Here you'll find some of the best views of Dublin around.

  4. Croke Park: Croke Park is the place to be for some of Ireland's most popular sporting and music events. The 82,300 person capacity stadium routinely welcomes top musical acts, like The Red Hot Chili Peppers and Dublin's own U2. But what really gets the turnstiles spinning are the national soccer showdowns and exciting hurling championships.

  5. St. Stephen's Green: There's a saying in Ireland, "If you don't like the weather, wait a few minutes." When the weather happens to be nice, St. Stephen's Green is a welcome refuge, with green gardens, duck ponds, walking paths and a sea of locals sprawled out on the grass and benches.
 
 

DUB: Dublin Airport


Dublin Airport is Ireland's international gateway, welcoming millions of visitors from around the world each year. Located just 6 miles north of Dublin, DUB offers easy access to the city center by bus, taxi or rental car. Open to commercial, private, charter and smaller aircraft, Dublin Airport is the busiest airport in all of Ireland.
