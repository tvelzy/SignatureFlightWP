This month: London

Whether you have visited London before or not, the city will always overwhelm you by its size (the largest city in Europe) and wealth of things to see and do. Each district offers something special: from the fashion boutiques and up-market stores of Covent Garden and the contemporary art galleries and major museums of Chelsea, Kensington and Knightsbridge, to the ever so charming Victorian architecture of Camden and nearby Hampstead Health, London's largest ancient parkland.

Things you can do

1 The Queen by Cecil Beaton at the V&A: As part of The Queen's Diamond Jubilee celebrations, the Victoria and Albert Museum (V&A) shows works by Cecil Beaton, the Queen's favorite photographer for over 40 years. Open 10 am - 5:45 pm daily and
10 am - 10 pm on a Friday.
2 The Tower of London: Be dazzled by the 23,578 gems that make up the Crown Jewels or discover five hundred years of spectacular royal armour. Open 9 am -
4:30 pm Tuesday to Saturday from and 10 am - 4:30 pm Sunday and Monday.
3 David Hockney at the Royal Academy: An unmissable exhibition of landscape works by renowned LA based British artist David Hockney, famous for his series of swimming pool paintings. Open 10 am - 6 pm daily and until 10 pm on a Friday.
4 The Phantom of the Opera in London: A chance to see the most spectacular, successful musical of all time. This legendary love story created by the genius Andrew Lloyd Webber takes place at Her Majesty's Theatre.
5 Dickens at the Museum of London: February 2012 marks the 200th anniversary of the birth of Charles Dickens. To celebrate the occasion, the Museum of London presents the first major exhibition on the influential author since 1970.
Open 10 am to 6 pm daily.
6 The Olympic Walks: As the excitement grows, why not take a sneak peek at how plans are coming along for the Games venues. Led by experienced and knowledgeable tour guides, the Olympic Walks is around 1.5 miles (2.4 km) to 2 miles (3.2 km) long and lasts around two hours.
