What's on in Des Moines

 

Planning on becoming President someday? Then Iowa's capital city of Des Moines should be your first stop! But if your higher office aspirations are on hold, this Midwestern gem should still be first on the list of places to visit.

Long known for its agricultural heritage and being a political bellwether, Des Moines is now a business and technology hotbed with a low cost of living and unmatched quality of life. And with an influx of young people bringing a new vibrancy to the historic downtown, Des Moines is experiencing a true cultural and economic renaissance. It's a city fit for a (future) President.

 
 
 
 

Top Attractions in Des Moines:

  1. Blank Park Zoo: Blank Park Zoo is one of the most popular attractions in Des Moines, and is the only accredited zoo in the state.

  2. Des Moines Art Center: The world of art comes to life in the halls of the Des Moines Art Center. In August, Henri Matisse's "Jazz" is on display.

  3. Principle RiverWalk: See the night skyline sparkle along this scenic pathway on the banks of the Des Moines River.

  4. Iowa State Capitol: Des Moines is the seat of Iowa state politics and home to one of the most ornate state capitol buildings in the country.
 
 

DSM: Des Moines International Airport


DSM is a public airport located just three miles southwest of Des Moines, serving the Midwest region with direct flights across the country. Des Moines International Airport offers easy access to several major thoroughfares including Interstate 35.
