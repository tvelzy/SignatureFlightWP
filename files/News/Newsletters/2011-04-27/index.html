This month: Washington, D.C.

Washington D.C. is truly breathtaking at this time of year. Spring has sprung and everything is lush and in bloom. The weather is at its best as it is warm enough to walk, run and bike around the many historic monuments, museums and parks and comfortable enough in the evenings to wear a sweater or light jacket.

Things you should know about Washington, D.C.

1 National Memorial Day: Free concert given by the National Symphony Orchestra on
  the West Lawn of the U.S. Capitol (Sunday, May 29, 2011, 8 p.m. Gates open at 5pm)
  and National Memorial Day Parade along Independence Avenue. May 30, 2011,
  beginning at 2pm.
2 Andrews AFB Airshow: Andrews Air Force Base, MD. USAF Thunderbirds pilots
  demonstrate their flight skills with aerial maneuvers. May 21-22, 2011.
3 Food & Wine Festival at National Harbor: Enjoy food and wine pairing, artisanal
  and organic products, lectures on culinary and wine trends and more. May 14-15,
  2011.
4 Europe Week: Events include speeches, seminars and workshops, film screenings,
  concerts, and cultural programs related to the European Union. May 7-13, 2011.

DCA: Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport

Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA) is located in Arlington, VA just across the Potomac River from the Nation's Capital and is directly linked to the region's Metrorail system which is adjacent to Terminal B/C.
