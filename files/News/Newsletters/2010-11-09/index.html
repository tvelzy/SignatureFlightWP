This month: Thessaloniki, Greece

Greece's second largest city, Thessaloniki is known not only for its major economic, industrial and political centers, but also for its commercial port that connects Greece to the Balkans and South East Europe. Thessaloniki has a regional and worldwide historical and commercial heritage that stretches over 2,300 years.

5 things you should know about Thessaloniki

1 Thessaloniki International Film Festival: Held every last quarter of the year, this
  film festival presents and honors the work of film directors from around the world in
  order to promote their masterpieces to the Greek market. December 3-12.
2 The Ancient Forum of Thessaloniki: This administrative center of ancient
  Thessaloniki once occupied almost two hectares of the old city. Its construction
  began at the end of the 2nd century A.D.
3 The White Tower: Dating back to the 15th century, this defensive structure had
  been used as a guards' quarters by the Janissaries and as a prison.
  No worries; today it's simply a historical museum.
4 Mount Athos: The famous mountain and peninsula located in Macedonia,
  Greece, draws big crowds as an UNESCO World Heritage Site.
5 Thessaloniki's active nightlife: This city is famous for its rich nightlife experience.
  The New York Times crowned Thessaloniki "Seattle of the Balkans".

SKG: Thessaloniki International Airport

Thessaloniki Airport is the second biggest airport in Greece, and is the largest state-owned airport in the country. It serves 4 million passengers per year and is located 14 km from the city center. Formerly known as Mikra Airport, it is close to the suburb of Peraia and the municipalities of Pylaia and Thermi.
