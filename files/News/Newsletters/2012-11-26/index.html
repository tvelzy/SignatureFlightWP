Holiday in New York City

During the holidays it is a great time to visit New York City. With its bustling streets, whether you're looking for window shopping, holiday events and performances, a place for holiday dinners, or to watch the ball drop, the city offers some of the most exciting joys of the holiday season. These seasonal favorites really heighten the holiday feeling that comes over you as you walk through the streets of Manhattan, during this time of year.
 
 
 

Things you can do:

  1. Fifth Avenue at Christmas: One of New York City's holiday traditions is the walk along Fifth Avenue from 59th Street to Macy's at 34th, where you will find the best festive sights. From the timeless Herald Angels in Rockefeller Center to the beautifully decorated holiday themed department store windows; there is so much to see.

  2. Rockefellar Center: Often known as a "city within a city", this cultural center in the heart of New York City is home to the world's most famous Christmas tree and widely filmed ice skating rink. This National landmark and "hub of Manhattan" is a great place to spend some time when visiting the city. Discover the ins and outs with a fantastic Rockefeller Center Tour. Tours are available daily at 10am. The ice Rink is open daily from 7am to Midnight.

  3. The Shops at Columbus Circle: This major landmark located in the soaring Time Warner Center and Central Park West, is a premier shopping destination featuring more than 40 retail shops. For art lovers, visit the ground floor's Metropolitan Museum of Art outpost which offers beautiful pieces similar to its namesake's shop. Open Monday to Saturday 10am to 9pm and Sunday 11am to 7pm.

  4. Horse-Drawn Carriage Rides in Central Park: A great way to experience the beauty of New York City during the holidays is by horse-drawn carriage. Rides range from 45 minutes to 1.5 hours and we suggest that you make an advance reservation. Rides are available during the holiday season
    (Nov 24 - Jan 5).

  5. FAO Schwarz: Founded in 1862, this Fifth Avenue flagship and popular tourist destination, is one of the oldest toy stores in the United States. Known for its special collection of overstuffed bears, collectible dolls, games, ornaments and iconic giant floor pianos that light up when stepped on, FAO Schwarz offers a fantasyland for both kids and adults alike. Special guided tours are also available. Open 9am to 8pm.

  6. New Year's Eve Ball Drop in Times Square: This is one of the greatest celebrations in town with a televised worldwide audience. Since 1907, every year at 11:59pm EST on December 31 the brilliant ball atop One Times Square in New York City is lowered to ring in the New Year. The fairy-tale-like ball, with its hundreds of LED lights and outer surface of triangle-shaped crystal panels, has been updated over the years to reflect new advances in technology. Join the millions of revelers who celebrate New Year's Eve in Times Square!
 
 

TEB: Teterboro Airport

Teterboro Airport: This private and corporate airport, located only 12 miles (19 km) from midtown Manhattan is one of the oldest operating airports in the New York City area. With twenty-three hangars (approximately 572,000 sq ft), this airport has an operations building, two fuel farms, aircraft rescue (ARFF) and maintenance facilities located at the airport. Getting to Teterboro is easy with buses, trains, limos and taxis, all operating on or near the airport and very much accessible by car via Route 46.
