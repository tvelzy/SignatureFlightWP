This month: Indianapolis, Indiana USA

A quintessential Midwestern city, Indianapolis is Indiana's state capitol and home to one of the most popular racecar events in the US, the Indianapolis 500®. With its temperate climate and an ever-so-slightly rolling terrain on the west fork of White River, the bustling city is the heart and soul of Indiana and the "Racing Capital of the World".

5 things you should know about Indianapolis

1 Indy 500®: On May 30, racecar fans will be angling for pole position to experience the
  94th Indianapolis 500-Mile Race, one of the top three motorsport events in the world.
2 War Memorial Plaza District: With the big race taking place on Memorial Day
  weekend, see how the locals celebrate by touring revered US war monuments.
3 White River State Park: Get your nature fix and amble through
  a charming mix of green spaces, performance venues and more.
4 Brickyard 400®: NASCAR® comes to Indianapolis with the running of three races July
  23-25. The NASCAR® Craftsman Truck series and NASCAR® Nationwide series are
  held at O'Riley Raceway Park and NASCAR® Sprint Cup series at the Indianapolis
  Motor Speedway.
5 Red Bull® Indianapolis GP®: The 2010 Red Bull® Indianapolis GP® will be Aug. 27-29 at the Indianapolis Motor Speedway®. Come and see the world's greatest motorcycleriders, such as Valentino Rossi and America's Colin Edwards,
  Nicky Hayden and Ben Spies! This is a must see for motorcycle fans everywhere.

IND - KIND: Indianapolis International Airport

Located just seven miles (11 km) southwest of the central business district of Indianapolis and the Indianapolis Motor Speedway®, this airport is the largest in Indiana and is the state's primary air passenger and cargo hub.
