What's on in Brasília

 

When flying into Brazil's capital city of Brasília, the first thing arriving visitors will notice from above is the unique landscape of this modern metropolis, distinctively shaped like an airplane. The city's aviation imagery is also symbolic, a testament to Brasília's soaring global ambitions. This June, the world's eyes will be on Brasília, a host city to one of the most exciting sport spectacles on the planet: the FIFA World Cup.

 
 
 
 

Top Attractions in Brasília:

  1. Pontão do Lago Sul: Situated on the edge of Lake Paranoá, Pontão do Lago Sul's beach-like atmosphere and scenic views make it the perfect spot to catch a Brazilian sunset or experience the pulsing energy of Brasília's nightlife.

  2. Palácio do Planalto: Brasília is renowned for its many buildings designed by modernist architect Oscar Niemeyer. One of his crown jewels is the 390,000 square foot Palácio do Planalto, home to the office of the President of Brazil.

  3. Mané Garrincha National Stadium: Brasília's brand-new 71,000-seat stadium has won praise for its gorgeous sight lines, state of the art amenities, and modern style. With construction completed just ahead of the World Cup, seven matches will be played here in June and July.

  4. Itiquira Falls: A visit to Brazil is not complete until you've seen one of the country's famed waterfalls. Just outside of Brasília is Itiquira Falls, a natural treasure featuring a 550-foot drop. Don't forget your bathing suit!

SBBR: Aeroporto Internacional de Brasília


Brasília International Airport is one of the busiest airports in Brazil, ranking fourth in number of transported passengers, third in terms of aircraft operations and fifth by amount of cargo handled in Brazil. With both the 2014 World Cup and 2016 Summer Olympics on the way, the airport is undergoing major upgrades to its passenger terminal, apron, taxiways and parking facility. It is ideally located just seven miles from downtown Brasília.
