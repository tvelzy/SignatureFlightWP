Palm Springs, California

The desert city of Palm Springs is an ideal location for outdoor activities – from hiking up canyons to hitting the links. Situated within the Coachella Valley, it is located 110 miles (179 km) east of Los Angeles and 268 miles (433 km) west of Phoenix, Arizona. In the 1920s, the city became a trendy resort location. Attracted by dry, sunny weather and seclusion, Hollywood stars arrived in droves. With its irresistible combination of natural beauty and temperate climes, it comes as no surprise that Fortune Magazine ranked Palm Springs ‘one of America’s legendary holiday destinations’.

 
 
 
 

Things you can do:

  1. San Jacinto Mountain Called the “smooth cliffs” by Cahuill Native Americans, this mountain range is one of the tallest in Southern California.  Hiking routes provide various levels of climbing: from the shortest route via the Marion Mountain Trail to the Cactus to Clouds trail that involves a demanding climb from the Palm Springs Art Museum to its peak.  Campground hours are open year-round.  Office Hours: Daily 8am to 4pm.

     

  2. Palm Springs Aerial Tramway: This aerial tram, one of the largest in the world, makes two complete revolutions giving passengers a spectacular 360° view that can stretch up to 200 mi (320 km) on a clear day. There are two restaurants at the summit, one of which specializes in fine dining. Cars depart every half hour. Monday to Friday 10 am to 9:45 pm.

  3. Palm Springs Art Museum: Formerly known as the Desert Museum, its main focus was once on the Cahuilla people, the Native American inhabitants of the region. Today this modern art structure has expanded to include an auditorium and additional galleries which house some of the most remarkable contemporary art exhibitions. Open Tuesday, Wednesday, Friday, Saturday and Sunday 10 am to 5 pm and Thursday 12 pm to 8 pm.

  4. VillageFest: This unique art and music fair is open to all that are interested in culture, exotic food and a great ambiance in the epicenter of beautiful downtown Palm Springs.  Open from October to May 6 pm to 10 PM and from June to September from 7 pm to 10 pm.

  5. Moorten Botanical Garden: Dedicated to cacti and the Colorado Desert ecosystem, this botanical garden is right in the heart of Canyon Drive.  A variety of the most amazing florae come from all over the world, including Baja California, mainland Mexico and even Guatemala.  Open from Monday to Sunday from 10 am to 4 pm and closed Wednesday.  

  6. Palm Indian Canyons: Enjoy the serenity of this remarkable fifteen-mile-long expansive area of beauty. Amidst the most amazing rock structures and barren desert lands, Palm Canyon is also home to lush plant and wildlife. Picnic near a stream or hike on foot or by horseback. Open Friday to Sunday from July to September from 8am to 5 pm, and open daily from October to June from 8 am to 5 pm.  

  7. Palm Springs Elite Land Tours: Relish a spectacular view of nature in an air-conditioned all-terrain vehicle accompanied by knowledgeable guides. These marvelous tours take you through the famous Joshua Tree National Park, which is considered to be one of the most important bird habitat areas in California.  Another option is the San Andreas Fault tour, which takes you over rocky hills to a dramatic section of the 800 mile fault and offers an incredible view of the Salton Sea.  Gourmet snacks and drinks are included. Open 7 days a week, 365 days a year.
 
 

PSP Palm Springs Intl Airport

Palm Springs International Airport: Featured in the Top 10 U.S ‘Most Stress-Free Airports’ by SmarterTravel.com, Palm Springs International Airport covers 940 acres and serves the Inland Metropolitan area of Southern California. PSP is served by eleven airlines and ten major airline hubs connecting to cities all over the world.  Signature Flight Support is located north of the terminal building where there are a range of aviation services available, including professional flight line service, gourmet inflight catering and limousine and car rentals. The airport is located 2 miles (3 km) from the central business district and is easily accessible by car and other forms of ground transportation.
