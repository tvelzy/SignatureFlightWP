What's on in Nashville

 

The Music City has come a long way since its humble country song beginnings. A vibrant metropolis where the lights shine bright and honky-tonk still rules, Nashville is enjoying a golden age of sorts, attracting new residents and tourists alike. Visitors can explore the hip nightlife scene and catch the "next big thing" at one of Nashville's many well-known entertainment venues. It's here that legends like Johnny Cash and Willie Nelson, as well as today's hottest stars like Brad Paisley and Lady Antebellum, got their start.

 
 
 
 

Top Attractions in Nashville:

  1. Grand Ole Opry House: This is the granddaddy of Nashville's live music scene. A stop in Nashville isn't complete until you see the Grand Ole Opry, the show that put country music on the map.

  2. Country Music Hall of Fame: Two-step your way through the halls of the Country Music Hall of Fame, where some of the greatest country singers to ever grace the stage are honored.

  3. Belle Meade Plantation: History comes alive on the grounds of this early 19th century plantation that now serves as a museum, winery and opulent event hall.

  4. Bridgestone Arena: Bridgestone welcomes rocking concerts, exciting shows and professional rodeos.

  5. Music Row: The heart and soul of Nashville's music industry, Music Row's streets are lined with music-related businesses, including the famed RCA Studio B recording studio.
 
 

BNA: Nashville International Airport


BNA is a public airport located in Southeast Nashville, just a short drive along Interstate 40 from downtown Nashville and other major attractions. As Nashville continues to grow, BNA is becoming an increasingly busy airport. Last year over 10 million passengers flew through the airport, making it the most trafficked year on record.
