What's on in Green Bay

 

Green Bay is an industrial city with a tightknit community located in northeast Wisconsin next to Lake Michigan. Of course the city's main claim to fame is hosting the NFL's Green Bay Packers - a team whose tradition, success and fan loyalty represent the spirit of its city. This team is the only community-owned professional sports franchise in US. The Packers gather more than 80,000 faithful fans from across the state for each home game, equal to more than 80% of the city's population. The Packers' home FBO is Jet Air - Signature Select™.

 

Every year, many aviation enthusiasts transit through Green Bay for the annual EAA AirVenture, an event frequently heralded as "The World's Greatest Aviation Celebration". The event will happen July 29th to August 4th at the Wittman Regional Airport in nearby Oshkosh, Wisconsin, which is 53 miles south of Green Bay's Austin Straubel International Airport.

 
 
 

Things you can do:

  1. Lambeau Field Tours: Experience the excitement of the 13-time world-champion Green Bay Packers by taking a guided tour of the team's celebrated stadium. The behind-the-scenes tour is a unique and unforgettable event for all football fans who seek to feel the energy of this All-American city.

  2. National Railroad Museum: This museum hosts an enormous collection of locomotives and railroad artifacts spanning the last century. On Saturday, July 13 and Sunday, July 14, the museum will host a WWII reenactment, where visitors can contemplate the war from the perspective of historic trains from the Allied and Axis powers and by touring the Eisenhower Train.

  3. Green Bay Botanical Garden: Canvass the 47-acre garden filled with diverse plants and architecture unique to the Upper Midwest. The garden will conduct its 25th Annual Garden Walk on Saturday, July 13 and Sunday, July 14, when all will be invited to walk private gardens only open to the public once each year.

  4. EAA AirVenture: This year's AirVenture in nearby Oshkosh, Wisconsin will feature a B-17 Flying Fortress which visitors can join for a flight. The show's more than 10,000 unique aircraft will make Wittman Regional Airport in Oshkosh the busiest airport in the world during the show. It will include air shows, aerobatics, pyrotechnics, Fly-In Theater and more. While attending the event, make sure to visit Signature Flight Support at booth 457.

  5. Fox River State Recreational Trail: Follow the Fox River by scenic trail, highlighting the richness of northeast Wisconsin, comprised of multiple routes going under the Mason Street Bridge and through agricultural areas.

  6. Bay Beach Amusement Park: Take a ride along the bay at the city's amusement park, scenically located by the water. Ride a Ferris wheel, a giant slide and the park's newest roller coaster Zippin Pippin - a 2,500-foot thrill ride.

 
 

GRB: Austin Straubel International Airport

Austin Straubel International Airport is located seven miles southwest of central Green Bay. The airport is home to Jet Air - Signature Select™, the fixed base operator used by the Green Bay Packers. The airport has two runways and four car rental companies on location.
