What's on in Denver

 

Located where the Great Plains end and the Rocky Mountains begin, Denver is truly one of America's most beautiful cities. Known for its high altitude and thin air, the "Mile High City" is a sprawling metropolis that appeals to both urbanites and outdoor adventurers alike.

Ideally located at the foot of the majestic Rockies, Denver has become a mecca for outdoor enthusiasts of all types. For those who like spending their free time hitting the slopes, Denver is a premier wintertime destination. With its close proximity to Vail, Aspen, Winter Park and many more of North America's top winter sports parks, snow lovers from around the globe flock here in search of fresh powder and the world's most challenging black diamond trails. Signature Flight Support offers two FBOs (DEN and APA) that give you easy access to the leading winter resorts without having to worry about a limited number of takeoff/landing slots.

 
 
 
 

Things you can do:

  1. Vail Ski Resort: Once upon a time, Vail was just a modest pasture whose population consisted of more sheep than people. Today, it's one of the most popular ski resorts in the world, and one of the largest. Vail came to be back in 1962, thanks to the vision of ski pioneers Pete Seibert and Earl Eaton. Since then, Vail has grown to over 190 runs and 5,289 acres of skiable area. The main commercial areas of the Town of Vail – Lionshead and Vail Village – both offer some of the best shopping and dining experiences in all of Colorado. Once you've visited Vail, it may be nearly impossible to ski or ride anywhere else.

  2. Aspen Mountain: Another legendary mountain resort, Aspen/Snowmass is famous for its spectacular beauty and challenging black diamond terrain. But what really sets Aspen apart is its luxury. For those looking to take a break from skiing and snowboarding, Aspen offers a slew of activities to please just about anyone. From guided powder tours aboard snowcats to morning yoga classes at the summit's Sundeck, Aspen know how to keep guests busy.

  3. Rocky Mountain National Park: Are you the indecisive type who wants to try everything? Well, Rocky Mountain National Park is the place for you. Located northwest of Denver, Rocky Mountain National Park offers a laundry list of outdoor activities for just about any interest. From scenic drives, to cross-country skiing, there is never a shortage of fun things to do in this sprawling wilderness. If you're looking for something truly unique, the park offers ranger-guided snowshoe programs.

  4. Mount Evans: The best part of any trip may be getting that one stunning photograph to share with all your family and friends. For the shutterbugs, there is no better spot then Mount Evans. But getting that special shot isn't easy. A trip here means traveling along one of the highest paved roads in North America, taking you nearly 9,000 feet up, through five climate zones to the 14,264-foot summit. During the snowy months, the road is closed for the 5-mile stretch to the summit, but the adventurous types can still hike or bike their way to the top.

  5. Glenwood Springs: Enough about all the outdoor stuff you can do. Let's talk about something a little more relaxing, namely Glenwood Springs and their bubbly pools. The legendary hot springs of Glenwood Springs have been visited by the likes of President Teddy Roosevelt and mobster Al Capone, to name a few. Visitors today can look forward to the unique experience of sitting in their bathing suits in the naturally heated springs, all while the flakes fall down around.

 
 

DEN: Denver International Airport

One of the busiest and largest airports in the United States, Denver International Airport is located just 20 minutes from downtown Denver, and is near such attractions as Coors Field, Mile High Stadium and the Pepsi Center. DEN is also within driving distance of some of the world's most famous ski reports, including Aspen, Vail and Breckenridge. One of the newest airports in the country, DEN features a spacious modern terminal, six runways and rental car facilities.

APA: Centennial Airport

Located immediately south of Denver in the City of Englewood, Centennial Airport is one of the nation's premier general aviation airports. Opened in 1963, APA has grown to become the third-busiest general aviation airport in the United States. An international airport with convenient access to all major highways, a trip to one of Colorado's many world famous ski resorts is easy. Airport features include three runways, 24-hour customs, a 24/7 FAA control tower and all weather capability.
