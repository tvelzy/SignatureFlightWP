What's on in Edmonton

 

Edmonton, the capital of the Canadian province of Alberta, is a cultural, governmental and educational center. It is known as Canada's Festival City, with over 30 annual gatherings, including North America's largest alternative theatre event. Edmonton is a tourism hot spot, boasting North America's largest mall and Canada's largest living history museum. It is also a haven for summer and winter activities, with 17 hours of daylight in mid-summer and the magical Northern Lights in peak winter.
 
 
 

Things you can do:

  1. High Level Bridge: In the summer, take a ride back into history on one of the highest river crossings by streetcar in the world and enjoy some of the best views of the city. Service begins in May and continues until October.

  2. West Edmonton Mall: Until as recently as 2004, this shopping center, spanning the equivalent of 48 city blocks, was listed in the Guinness Book of World Records as the largest mall in the world. With over 800 stores and services, it is home to the world's second-largest indoor waterpark and an entertainment complex consisting of one of Edmonton's major music venues.

  3. Castrol Raceway: For those with an adventurous side, Edmonton's Castrol Raceway will help you put the pedal to the metal. Purchase a day ticket to enjoy the sights and sounds, or check out one of several races scheduled for May.

  4. Stroll down 124th Street: Grab a latte and walk down trendy 124th Street or Whyte Avenue in Old Strathcona – voted one of Canada's Top 10 coolest neighborhoods.

  5. Edmonton Festivals: Canada's Festival City has no fewer than five festivals planned for May, ranging from dance to music to First Nations' performing arts.

  6. Art Gallery of Alberta: Having undergone a dramatic exterior design upgrade which has attracted national attention, this Museum of Art includes a gallery shop, 150-seat theatre and an espresso bar and restaurant offering stunning views of Edmonton's eclectic downtown.

  7. Edmonton River Valley: Walk along the North Saskatchewan River on one of the numerous park trails and take in nature at its finest. This is one of the largest urban stretches of river valley parkland in North America, so there is plenty to enjoy.
 
 

CYEG: Edmonton International Airport

 

Edmonton International Airport is approaching its final stage of the 2012 expansion project. The new US Departures Lounge and combined office and control tower (Central Tower) both open this year, improving airside ground operations. This expansion includes the opening of the Renaissance by Mariott Hotel that will offer more convenience to business passengers, featuring comfortable overnight lodging at the airport. Located only 16 miles from downtown Edmonton, taxis, limo service and ground transport are readily available via EIA Express Route 747.
