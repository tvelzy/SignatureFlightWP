What's on in Fort Worth

 

Many consider Forth Worth a little sister to its larger neighbor Dallas, but visitors to Fort Worth will enjoy a city rich in heritage and a gateway to the American Southwest. Fort Worth's 750,000 residents preserve its history, with nicknames like "Cow Town" and "Where the West Begins."

 

Fort Worth celebrates its wild-frontier history and continues its cowboy traditions while also priding itself on urban renewal and cultural arts. Attractions include a daily cattle drive and the world's largest honky-tonk, along with performing arts venues which house symphony orchestras, operas and musicals.

 

 

 

Things you can do:

  1. American Airlines CR Smith Museum: See a Douglas DC-3 airliner and relish the history of aviation and its development over the last century. The museum's interactive exhibits display artifacts, full-scale aircraft engines and aerial photography.

  2. Billy Bob's Texas: Fort Worth's world-famous western night club is known as "The World's Largest Honky Tonk" with 30 bar stations and a capacity of over 6,000. The club will hold concerts of well-known artists Friday and Saturday nights throughout September. These performances will add to the rich tradition of country stars who have sang and recorded albums on the club's stages - such as superstar singers Willie Nelson, George Strait and Alan Jackson.

  3. Bass Performance Hall: Visit the new $65 million concert hall acclaimed as the "crown jewel of downtown Fort Worth." This European-style opera house contains a 2,056-seat theater praised for its acoustics and collection of art deco posters inside, along with an 80-foot dome and limestone angel sculptures that grace the exterior. Bass Hall will show 12 events in September, including "An Evening with Bill Cosby" September 24.

  4. Stockyards Championship Rodeo: Be entertained by Fort Worth's rodeo any Friday or Saturday night in September at the lively indoor Cowtown Coliseum. Some of the rodeo's spectacles include bull riding, barrel racing and calf roping.

  5. Kimbell Art Museum: Fort Worth's premier art museum features works from famous international artists. View revolutionary originals by Pablo Picasso, Henri Matisse and Marcel Duchamp throughout September in the museum's exhibit The Age of Picasso and Matisse: Modern Masters from The Art Institute of Chicago.

  6. Fort Worth Herd: Gain a glimpse of Old West history and see gorgeous Texas longhorns during authentic re-enactments of 19th-century cattle drives depicted daily at Stockyards Station.

 

 

FTW: Fort Worth Meacham International Airport

 

Meacham International Airport is located about 10 miles from downtown Fort Worth, in the northwest part of the city, and some 40 miles from the center of Dallas. The airport serves private and small corporate airline travel. It has three runways and rental car facilities, as well as housing Signature Select location American Aero FTW.
